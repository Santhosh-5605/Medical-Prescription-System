Cough Information

Overview

Coughing is a common respiratory symptom that can be caused by various factors, including infections, allergies, or irritants. It is the body's way of clearing the airways.

Common Causes

  • Viral Infections (e.g., common cold, flu)
  • Bacterial Infections (e.g., bronchitis, pneumonia)
  • Allergies (e.g., pollen, dust)
  • Environmental Irritants (e.g., smoke, pollution)
  • Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)

Medication

If you have a persistent cough, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional for a proper diagnosis and treatment plan. Medications that may be prescribed include:

  • Cough Suppressants
  • Expectorants
  • Antibiotics (if the cough is due to a bacterial infection)
  • Antihistamines (if allergies are the cause)

Suggestions

Here are some general suggestions for managing a cough:

  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids.
  • Use a humidifier to add moisture to the air.
  • Avoid irritants like smoking or exposure to secondhand smoke.
  • Get plenty of rest to help your body heal.
  • If the cough persists or worsens, seek medical advice.

Remember, it's crucial to follow your healthcare provider's recommendations for the most effective treatment.
